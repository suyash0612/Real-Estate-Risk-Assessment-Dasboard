Real Estate & Investment Terminology

Cap Rate
Capitalization Rate
Annual net income divided by property value. Shows return on investment. Higher cap rates indicate higher income relative to property price, often in riskier markets.
DSCR
Debt Service Coverage Ratio
Net operating income divided by annual debt payment. Shows ability to cover loan payments. DSCR > 1.25 is typically required by lenders; below 1.0 means negative cash flow.
Cash-on-Cash Return
Annual cash flow / down payment
The percentage return on the actual cash you put down. If you put $20k down and earn $2k/year, your CoC is 10%.
ARV
After-Repair Value
The estimated market value of a property after renovations. Critical for BRRRR and fix-and-flip strategies. Must be backed by comparable sales.
BRRRR
Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat
A real estate investment strategy where you buy below-market properties, improve them, rent them out, refinance at higher value, and repeat.
LTV
Loan-to-Value Ratio
The loan amount divided by property value. A 20% down payment = 80% LTV. Lower LTV = less risk for lender and you have more equity cushion.
NOI
Net Operating Income
Rental income minus all operating expenses (taxes, insurance, maintenance, property management). Excludes mortgage principal and income taxes.
HHI Index
Herfindahl-Hirschman Index
Measures economic concentration by employer. High HHI means one or few employers dominate, increasing regional economic risk.
Vacancy Rate
% of units unoccupied / not generating rent
Measures rental market tightness. 5% is healthy; 10%+ is stressed. We stress-test your cash flow at 15%, 20%, 25% vacancy levels.
CoC Return
Cash-on-Cash Return (annual %)
Annual net cash flow divided by total cash invested (down payment + closing + reserves). Real measure of investor's actual return.
Capex Reserve
Capital Expenditure Reserve Fund
Money set aside for major repairs (roof, HVAC, foundation). Rule of thumb: 10-20% of annual rent. We flag if your reserve is insufficient.

Pro Tips for Using This Platform

  • Start with the Quiz: Your investor type shapes which metrics matter most. A BRRRR investor cares about ARV and refi potential; a buy-and-hold cares about DSCR.
  • Use the Underwriter for Comparison: Analyze multiple properties side-by-side to understand how location, price, and condition affect returns.
  • Stress Test Conservatively: Don't just model base-case scenarios. Test what happens if vacancy hits 20%, rent drops 10%, or rates jump 2%.
  • Review Your Persona's Checklist: Each investor type has a custom input checklist. Use it as a due-diligence checklist before making an offer.
